<div><h1 class="headline">Severe weather coming to Texas, Southeast</h1><p>Fox News Flash top headlines are here. Check out what's clicking on Foxnews.com.</p>
<p class="speakable">An intense storm system developing across the Central U.S. will bring the risk of widespread severe weather this week.  </p>
<p class="speakable">An area of low pressure coming out of the Rockies will begin to strengthen from Texas along the Gulf Coast and into the Southeast.  </p>
<p>
      The severe storm threat for Monday.
      </p>
<p>Heavy rain, large hail, damaging winds and tornadoes will all potentially affect tens of millions of people in this area.  </p>
<p></p>
<p>
      Where the severe weather is heading in the coming days.
      </p>
<p>Tuesday and Wednesday will be the most dangerous day and residents should have a way to receive the latest storm alerts. </p>
<p>Meanwhile, it will be cold enough for snow on the west side of this system bringing moderate to heavy totals from the Rockies into the Plains.</p>
<p>
      The national forecast for Monday, March 21.
      </p>
<p></p>
<p>Fire weather will also be elevated for parts of western Texas. </p>
<p>The Northwest will get another round of rain and mountain snow with warmer temperatures moving in on Tuesday. </p>
